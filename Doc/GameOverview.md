# Game Overview

## Type of Game

A digital tactical card-and-board game for **1–4 players**, combining strategic positioning, deck management, and turn-based combat.

### Key Features

* 1–4 players
* Turn-based gameplay
* Tactical grid-based movement
* Card-driven combat system
* Local-hosted multiplayer (one player hosts the game server)
* Playable in PvP, co-op, or solo modes depending on game mode

### Inspirations

* Unmatched
* Disney Lorcana
* Sunderfolk
* Teamfight Tactics

---

# Core Objective

Players control unique Champions and use cards, movement, and abilities to defeat opponents or complete scenario objectives. Success depends on positioning, action management, hand management, and adapting to changing turn order.

---

# Champion System

At the start of the match, each player selects one of four pre-designed Champions.

Each Champion has:

* A unique ability
* A distinct playstyle
* Different health values
* Different attack and defense strengths and movement

The goal is to create asymmetrical gameplay while maintaining overall balance.

## Health

Champions begin the game with health ranging from **16 to 20 HP**.

| Champion Archetype | Starting HP |
| ------------------ | ----------- |
| Assassin           | 16          |
| Specialist         | 17          |
| Fighter            | 18          |
| Guardian           | 20          |

## Combat Affinity

Champions determine how effectively card values are used.

### Attack Cap

Depending on the Champion:

* Maximum attack values may be 4, 5, or 6.

### Defense Cap

Depending on the Champion:

* Maximum defense values may be 4, 5, or 6.

This allows some Champions to specialize in offense while others excel defensively.

## Unique Abilities

Each Champion possesses a unique active or passive ability that defines their role.

Examples:

* Increased mobility
* Bonus defenses
* Additional card draw
* Damage amplification
* Support or utility effects

---

# Match Structure

A match is divided into rounds.

## Round Start

1. Randomly determine player order.
2. Players take turns according to the generated order.

Turn order is rerolled every round to keep gameplay dynamic and prevent fixed initiative advantages.

---

# Turn Structure

During their turn, a player:

1. Draws 1 card.
2. Receives their available actions.
3. Performs actions in any order.
4. Resolves end-of-turn effects.
5. Discards down to the hand limit if necessary.
6. Ends their turn.

---

# Action System

Players normally receive **2 actions per turn** and **1 action outsite their turn for round**, though Champion abilities, cards, or game effects may increase or reduce this amount.

Actions may be spent in any combination.

## Movement

**Cost:** 1 Action

* Move up to 3 squares on the board, depending of the character.
* Movement may be split across multiple actions.

Examples:

* Move 3 squares once.
* Move 6 squares by spending 2 actions.

## Attack

**Cost:** 1 Action

* Attack a player or enemy within the required range.
* Attack strength is determined by cards and Champion modifiers.

## Special Actions

Additional actions may become available through:

* Champion abilities
* Card effects
* Scenario mechanics
* Environmental interactions

### Action Freedom

Players may:

* Move twice
* Attack twice
* Move then attack
* Attack then move
* Mix actions freely
* Choose not to spend all available actions

---

# Deck & Hand System

## Deck

Each player uses a personal deck containing ** 45 - 50 cards**.

## Card Draw

At the start of every turn:

* Draw 1 card.
* Drawing is mandatory.

## Hand Limit

Start game hand size: **5 cards**
Maximum hand size: **7 cards**

## Overflow Rule

Players may temporarily exceed 7 cards during their turn.

Before ending their turn:

* If they have more than 7 cards in hand, they must discard cards until they have 7 or fewer.
* A turn cannot end while above the hand limit.
* If they taske more than 30 seconds to discard the cards, the game discards them randomly. 

This creates meaningful hand-management decisions rather than preventing draws entirely.

# Card System

Cards represent offensive, defensive, and tactical options.

## Attack Cards

Contain an Attack value between **1 and 6**.

## Defense Cards

Contain a Defense value between **1 and 6**.

## Hybrid Cards

Contain both Attack and Defense values.

## Ability Cards

May provide additional effects such as:

* Extra movement
* Additional actions
* Healing
* Shields
* Damage bonuses
* Status effects
* Position swapping
* Forced movement
* Card draw
* Buffs and debuffs

## Instant Cards

This cards use 1 action and can be used whenever. They can provide effects such as :

* Add more actions
* Draw X cards
* Health
* Cancel effects
This cards, if told so, can be used on themselves or others.

# Tactical Board Gameplay

Players occupy spaces on a grid-based battlefield.

Positioning is a critical part of gameplay because movement and attack opportunities are limited.

Players must consider:

* Distance from enemies
* Threat ranges
* Escape routes
* Objective control
* Team positioning
* Board hazards and obstacles

Good positioning can be as important as playing the right card.

---

# Resource Management

Players must constantly manage:

* Health
* Hand size
* Card usage
* Board position
* Available actions

The interaction between these resources forms the game's strategic depth.

# Strategic Pillars

### Champion Identity

Each Champion offers a distinct playstyle through unique abilities and stat profiles.

### Tactical Positioning

Success depends on controlling space and movement across the board.

### Action Economy

Players must maximize the value of their limited actions every turn.

### Hand Management

Choosing which cards to keep and which to discard is a constant strategic decision.

### Adaptability

Randomized turn order and card draw ensure that players must continually adapt their plans.

Draw cards each turn, manage a limited hand, and spend actions on movement, attacks, and powerful abilities. With randomized turn order, asymmetrical Champions, and strategic board positioning, every match requires careful planning and adaptation.
